Statistical Flaws in Raw Dataset Uncontrolled Baseline
Three core structural distortions that create artificial post-2014 skews in raw scraped web data.
1
Pre-2014 Media Left-Truncation
Scrapers miss regional print media (*Dainik Jagran*, *Amar Ujala*, *Eenadu*) prior to 2012, recording only 23 pre-2014 baseline leaks vs 86 post-2014 entries.
2
Post-2014 Reporting Drift & Noise
Post-2014 entries capture social media claims, Telegram hoaxes, and disproven allegations (19.1% noise: 21 claims out of 110 records), whereas pre-2014 includes higher-severity incidents with formal police/court documentation.
3
Federal Domain Misattribution
79.8% of confirmed incidents in this dataset (71/89) were assigned to state-level institutions under State PSCs and Boards, but raw datasets attribute them to Union Government UPA/NDA eras.

Dataset Citation & Origins: Sujay Nadkarni's Kaggle Baseline
This audit builds upon the original baseline dataset compiled by Sujay Nadkarni on Kaggle titled "India Paper Leaks from 2004 to 2026". Nadkarni's open-source initiative was among the first public attempts to aggregate web-scraped online news coverage of paper leak allegations in India. While invaluable as a starting point, web-scraping digital news APIs inherently creates temporal left-truncation (missing un-indexed pre-2012 regional print media) and post-2014 social media noise (including unverified Telegram claims). Our project audits Nadkarni's original 110-incident baseline by standardizing state executive tenure, modeling state baseline risk fixed-effects, and applying severity noise filtering.

Layer 3: Methodology & Statistical Cleaning Guide

A step-by-step accounting of the mathematical definitions, rate adjustments, and risk controls applied in this study.

1
Level-1 Confirmed Severity Filter
Pre-2014 entries in web scrapers represent a "High-Severity Only" tail (cases leading to CBI probes or court convictions). Post-2014 data captures social media claims, Telegram hoaxes, and disproven allegations (19.1% noise: 21 claims removed). We filter strictly for **Level-1 Confirmed Leaks** (official cancellations, re-tests, FIRs) to establish an un-biased baseline between eras.
Filter: leak_status == 'Confirmed' (Noise = 21 claims removed)
2
Pre-2014 Digital Left-Truncation Audit
Due to the 2004–2012 regional print media "digital dark age" (un-indexed local pullouts of regional newspapers), pre-2014 entries suffer from search API left-truncation. Our audit evaluates the 23 authenticated pre-2014 baseline incidents (PL-0002 to PL-0024) against post-2014 reporting to correct left-truncation skew.
Pre-2014 Baseline Sample: 23 Authenticated Leaks (UPA Era)
3
Party Executive Tenure Normalization
Comparing raw leak counts by political party (e.g. BJP: 38 vs INC: 14) introduces **exposure bias** because the BJP held state executive power across more states for longer aggregate durations post-2014 (207.8 State-Years for BJP vs 187.9 for INC). We divide confirmed leaks by cumulative **State-Years in Power** (1 State-Year = 1 state governed for 1 year).
Tenure Rate = Confirmed Leaks / Cumulative State-Years (2004–2026)
4
Stratified Baseline Risk Standardization (O / E)
High-density examination states (UP: 0.496 leaks/yr, MP: 0.451/yr, Rajasthan: 0.406/yr) have inherently higher baseline leak proclivities. We calculate **Expected Leaks (E)** under a neutral null model and compare with **Observed Leaks (O)**. Ratios of BJP (1.10) vs INC (0.83) demonstrate performance near state baseline expectations.
Expected E_party = ∑ (State_Baseline_Risks × Years_Governeds)
5
Level-4 Exam Conduct Volume Exposure Normalization
Controlling for time in office (Level 2) and state risk (Level 3) leaves **examination conduct opportunity exposure** uncontrolled. Following the creation of autonomous state selection boards (RSMSSB 2014, UPSSSC 2014, HSSC portal) and multi-shift CBT paper set multiplication, exam conduct volume expanded by **2.2× post-2014**. We normalize confirmed leaks per 1,000 major exams conducted.
Volume Rate = (Confirmed State Leaks / Total Est. Major Exams) × 1,000 (BJP 1.29 vs INC 1.07; RR = 1.20)

Each state's baseline risk rate (Rs) is calculated as: Rs = Total Confirmed State Leaks / 22.16 Years. A party's Expected Leaks (E) is the sum of its governing years in each state multiplied by that state's baseline risk rate.

Layer 4: Compiled Statistical Takeaways & Accuracy Comparison
📊 Statistical Significance Guide: How to Read Confidence Intervals (CIs)
Why do Confidence Intervals Matter? Point estimates (like O/E = 1.10 vs 0.83) carry random sampling variation due to small event counts (38 vs 14 leaks). The 95% Poisson Confidence Interval for the Stratified Risk Ratio is [0.72, 2.44] and for Consolidated Risk is [0.62, 2.11].
  • The Core Statistical Rule: Whenever a 95% Confidence Interval spans across 1.00 (e.g. 0.72 ≤ 1.00 ≤ 2.44), the observed difference is NOT statistically significant at conventional levels (p > 0.05).
  • Plain-English Reader Takeaway: The controlled dataset does NOT prove one political party is more leak-prone than another. Both parties execute near expected state baseline risk, and remaining differences sit within expected random variation.

❌ Flawed Raw Dataset Takeaway: "NDA era saw a massive 3.6x surge in paper leaks (86 vs 23 cases), proving an administrative collapse post-2014."
✓ Controlled Econometric Takeaway: "For broad examination-integrity events, confirmed rates are 5.35 vs 2.30 events/yr (Central: 0.90 vs 0.60 events/yr). Isolating narrow Question-Paper Leaks yields 3.87 vs 1.10 leaks/yr, while Central Question-Paper Leaks achieve virtual parity: NDA-II 0.58 leaks/yr (7 in 12.2 yrs) vs UPA 0.50 leaks/yr (5 in 10.0 yrs)."
Why Controlled View is More Accurate: Raw web scrapers confuse broad cheating scandals with paper leaks and collapse central/state jurisdictions. Lingering Data Limitations & Methodological Constraints (Post-Cleaning)

Despite rigorous econometric cleaning and archival expansion, statistical models inherently retain structural limitations that readers should consider.

⚠️ 1. Residual Pre-2010 Regional Print Dark Age
District pullouts from small regional-language newspapers prior to 2010 remain partially un-digitized, causing residual temporal left-truncation in media search APIs. Residual left-truncation may still exist for minor district-level recruitment tests prior to 2010.
⚠️ 2. Reporting Asymmetry vs. True Incident Frequency
Public leak datasets measure reported and acknowledged breaches, not necessarily total undetected breaches. In tightly controlled or high-coercion administrative settings, compromised exams that were never officially investigated or cancelled remain absent from all media datasets.
⚠️ 3. Social Media Amplification Drift Asymmetry
Post-2020 leak allegations spread instantaneously across Telegram, WhatsApp, and YouTube channels, creating rapid public pressure for official cancellations. Pre-2010 leaks lacked instant viral amplification, meaning minor leaks may have been resolved locally without national media coverage.
⚠️ 4. Mid-Term Coalition Shifting Attribution
In states with frequent mid-term coalition shifts (e.g. Maharashtra, Bihar), attributing a leak to a specific party requires exact date-matching against sworn cabinet dates. While our dataset maps exact incident dates to executive ruling parties, brief transition periods introduce minor attribution edge cases.
⚠️ 5. Non-Quantified Economic & Aspirant Costs
This study quantifies incident frequency and risk ratios, but does not quantify candidate financial loss, mental health tolls, coaching cartel revenue skews, or state re-test administrative expenses.

Layer 6: Speculative Underreporting Imputation Model

Note: This sandbox section applies Poisson media-truncation decay models and Lincoln-Petersen Capture-Recapture DSE without modifying empirical facts in Layers 1–5.

To address the residual pre-2012 regional print media dark age, this speculative layer applies two standard econometric techniques—Poisson Digital Decay Imputation and Capture-Recapture Dual-System Estimation (DSE)—to estimate un-digitized regional paper leaks.

📐 Technique 1: Poisson Media-Truncation Decay Imputation
Models digital search API decay prior to 2012 using a calibrated logistic capture probability function: P(Digital Index | t) = 1 / (1 + e^-0.45(t - 2012)) (where λ = 0.45 and t₀ = 2012 are calibrated to regional print digitization inflection rates). Imputes ~1.4 additional regional leaks/year for pre-2012 years, scaling historical UPA leaks from 23 observed to 40 estimated true leaks.
🔍 Technique 2: Lincoln-Petersen Capture-Recapture DSE
Estimates unobserved leak populations by analyzing overlap between independent data lists (Media Reports List M = 40 vs State Court Writs List C = 35; Overlap R = 27): N = ((M+1)(C+1) / (R+1)) - 1 = (41 × 36 / 28) - 1 = 51.7. Derives an estimated 1.45× underreporting multiplier for district-level recruitment tests.
